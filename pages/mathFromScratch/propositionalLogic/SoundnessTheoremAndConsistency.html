2.6 Soundness Theorem and Consistency

To prove that \(P_2\)'s theorems are necessarily tautologies, we need to do two things:

  • Base case: show that all axiom schemes are tautologies
  • Inductive case: show that from two tautologies, the Modus Ponens inference rule can only produce another tautology

Behold the truth tables for each axiom scheme:

  • \(A_1\): \(\varphi \rightarrow (\psi \rightarrow \varphi)\)
    \(\varphi\) \(\psi\) \(\psi \rightarrow \varphi\) \(\varphi \rightarrow (\psi \rightarrow \varphi)\)
    \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
  • \(A_2\): \([\varphi\rightarrow(\psi\rightarrow\eta)]\rightarrow[(\varphi\rightarrow\psi)\rightarrow(\varphi\rightarrow\eta)]\)
    \(\varphi\) \(\psi\) \(\eta\) \(\psi \rightarrow \eta\) \(\varphi \rightarrow \psi\) \(\varphi \rightarrow \eta\) \(\varphi \rightarrow (\psi \rightarrow \eta)\) \((\varphi \rightarrow \psi) \rightarrow (\varphi \rightarrow \eta)\) \(A_2\)
    \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
  • \(A_3\): \((\neg\varphi\rightarrow\neg\psi)\rightarrow(\psi\rightarrow\varphi)\)
    \(\varphi\) \(\psi\) \(\neg\varphi\) \(\neg\psi\) \(\neg\varphi\rightarrow\neg\psi\) \(\psi \rightarrow \varphi\) \((\neg\varphi\rightarrow\neg\psi)\rightarrow(\psi\rightarrow\varphi)\)
    \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)
    \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{F}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\) \(\mathcal{T}\)

We do indeed observe that each entry of the last column is true and this proves the base case.

To prove the inductive case, assume that \(\varphi\) and \(\varphi \rightarrow \psi\) are true. Since \(\varphi\) is true, \(\varphi \rightarrow \psi\) can only be true whenever \(\psi\) is true. Thus, \(\psi\), the theorem produced by Modus Ponens applied to \(\varphi\) and \(\varphi \rightarrow \psi\), is necessarily true.

∎

This metatheorem tells us that a wff isn't provable if it isn't a tautology. If A wff is a tautology though, we aren't yet sure whether it's provable. That's what we'll be aiming to prove with the completeness theorem.
